About

I currently work as a consultant within the Volvo Buses' Service Market Technology department, where I hold the role of a 'Technical Information Specialist.' My daily mission revolves around the creation of diverse documentation that explains how components and systems work. My commitment lies in presenting this complex technical information to our mechanics in a manner that is not only user-friendly but also effortlessly comprehensible.

Personal contribution

My main role is creating "Design and Function" documentation, which involves meticulous research, accurate content creation, and document formatting using an XML editor to enable internal database publishing and multilingual translation. I focus on terminology, descriptions, and safety warnings so that the documentation is both informative and user-friendly for engineers and others who rely on it.

An overview of the typical process for creating such documentation is presented below.

Step 1: Research

  • Gather written information about various system components.
  • Explore technical requirements and specifications.
  • Conduct interviews with experienced service engineers for valuable insights.

Step 2: Content Creation

  • Craft a comprehensive document based on gathered information.
  • Ensure accurate terminology and descriptions throughout by getting feedback from my colleagues.
  • Pay special attention to including essential notes and warnings, particularly those related to safety precautions.

Step 3: Document Format

  • Create and structure the document using our XML editor.
  • This format ensures compatibility with the internal database.
  • It also facilitates the translation of the document into multiple required languages, enhancing accessibility for a broader audience.
